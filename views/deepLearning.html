What is Deep Learning?

Neuroplacticity

Let's first take a look at how the human brain works.The human brain contains billions of neurons. As people learn new things, electrical pulses fire through links in-between neurons making those connections/paths stronger and changing the way people think. This allows people to change and adapt to their surroundings.

ANN: Artificial Neural Networks

ANN is based off the same design as a human brain's neural network. These networks are made up of linked nodes that connect information.

What if we tried to teach a Deep Learning AI what a dog is?

Since a computer is mainly limited to visuals and sound, the perfect input would be pictures or videos of what a dog looked and sounded like. The computer could then analyze these pictures and videos and store data about the distinct features of a dog itself without any human help. The computer would basically create its own definition of what makes a dog a dog.

Deep Learning relies on large amounts of labled data

If we just give the computer a picture of a dog, it won't know what it is. We still have to tell the computer that it's a dog. Similar to a young child learning about the world, a child won't know what a dog is until someone else teaches it to them.
So the computer is not completely independent and still relies on human input to learn what new things are.
